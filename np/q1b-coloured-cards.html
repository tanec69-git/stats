Probability of Drawing Coloured Cards

Problem

In a deck of 10 coloured cards:
🟥

Red Cards

3 cards

🟦

Blue Cards

2 cards

🟨

Yellow Cards

5 cards

🎴

Total Cards

10 cards


If 3 cards are drawn randomly, find the probability of getting:
  1. Three red cards
  2. One blue and two yellow cards

(i) Three Red Cards

Choose all 3 cards from the 3 red cards.
\[ P(\text{3 red cards}) = \frac{{^3C_3}}{{^{10}C_3}} \]
\[ = \frac{1}{120} \]
Probability = 1/120

(ii) One Blue and Two Yellow Cards

Choose:

1 blue card from 2 blue cards

and

2 yellow cards from 5 yellow cards
\[ P(\text{1 blue and 2 yellow}) = \frac{ {^2C_1}\times{^5C_2} } { {^{10}C_3} } \]
\[ = \frac{2 \times 10}{120} \]
\[ = \frac{20}{120} = \frac{1}{6} \]
Probability = 1/6
