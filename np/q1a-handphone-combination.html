Find the number of ways John can buy 6 handphones if:
  1. There are no restrictions
  2. All the phones must be Apple phones
  3. He did not want any Huawei phones
  4. He wants exactly 3 Samsung phones
  5. He wants at least 2 Huawei phones

(i) No Restrictions

Choose any 6 phones from 17 phones.
\[ ^{17}C_6 \]
\[ = 12376 \]
12376 ways

(ii) All Apple Phones

Choose all 6 phones from the 9 Apple phones.
\[ ^{9}C_6 \]
\[ = 84 \]
84 ways

(iii) No Huawei Phones

Choose 6 phones from Apple and Samsung phones only.

\[ 9 + 5 = 14 \]
\[ ^{14}C_6 \]
\[ = 3003 \]
3003 ways

(iv) Exactly 3 Samsung Phones

Choose:

3 Samsung phones from 5

and

3 other phones from 12 non-Samsung phones
\[ ^{5}C_3 \times \, ^{12}C_3 \]
\[ = 10 \times 220 \]
\[ = 2200 \]
2200 ways

(v) At Least 2 Huawei Phones

Possible cases:

• 2 Huawei phones

• 3 Huawei phones

Use the Addition Principle.

\[ ^{3}C_2 \times \, ^{14}C_4 + ^{3}C_3 \times \, ^{14}C_3 \]
\[ = (3 \times 1001) + (1 \times 364) \]
\[ = 3003 + 364 \]
\[ = 3367 \]
3367 ways
