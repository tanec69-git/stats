The probability of a secondary school student taking Geography is 0.36, the probability of taking History is 0.27, and the probability of taking both Geography and History is 0.12.

Find the probability that the student takes:

(i) either Geography or History, or both

(ii) either Geography or History, but not both

(iii) neither Geography nor History

(i) Either Geography or History, or both

Use the formula: \[ P(G \cup H) = P(G) + P(H) - P(G \cap H) \] Substitute the values: \[ = 0.36 + 0.27 - 0.12 \] \[ = 0.51 \] Therefore: \[ P(G \cup H) = 0.51 \]

(ii) Either Geography or History, but not both

First find the Geography only region: \[ 0.36 - 0.12 = 0.24 \] Then find the History only region: \[ 0.27 - 0.12 = 0.15 \] Add both regions: \[ 0.24 + 0.15 \] \[ = 0.39 \] Therefore: \[ P(\text{Geography or History, but not both}) = 0.39 \]

(iii) Neither Geography nor History

Use the complement rule: \[ P(\text{Neither}) = 1 - P(G \cup H) \] Substitute the value: \[ = 1 - 0.51 \] \[ = 0.49 \] Therefore: \[ P(\text{Neither}) = 0.49 \]
