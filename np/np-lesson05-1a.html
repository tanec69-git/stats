Given \( P(A)=0.25 \) and \( P(B)=0.38 \), where A and B are mutually exclusive, find:

(i) \( P(A') \)
(ii) \( P(B') \)
(iii) \( P(A \cup B) \)
(iv) \( P(A' \cap B') \)

(i) \( P(A') \)

Since complement means "not A":

\[ P(A') = 1 - P(A) \] \[ = 1 - 0.25 \] \[ = 0.75 \]

(ii) \( P(B') \)

\[ P(B') = 1 - P(B) \] \[ = 1 - 0.38 \] \[ = 0.62 \]

(iii) \( P(A \cup B) \)

Since A and B are mutually exclusive: \[ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) \] \[ = 0.25 + 0.38 \] \[ = 0.63 \]

(iv) \( P(A' \cap B') \)

\( A' \cap B' \) means neither A nor B.

Therefore: \[ P(A' \cap B') = 1 - P(A \cup B) \] \[ = 1 - 0.63 \] \[ = 0.37 \]
