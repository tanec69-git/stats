Mutually Exclusive Events Visualizer

Given:
\[ P(X)=0.30 \] \[ P(Y)=0.42 \] \[ P(X \cup Y)=0.72 \]
Determine whether events \( X \) and \( Y \) are mutually exclusive.

Explain your answer.

Step 1: Use the Addition Formula

For any two events:
\[ P(X \cup Y) = P(X)+P(Y)-P(X \cap Y) \]
Substitute the given values: \[ 0.72 = 0.30 + 0.42 - P(X \cap Y) \]

Step 2: Simplify

First add: \[ 0.30 + 0.42 = 0.72 \] Hence: \[ 0.72 = 0.72 - P(X \cap Y) \] Rearranging: \[ P(X \cap Y)=0 \]

Step 3: Conclusion

Two events are mutually exclusive if:
\[ P(X \cap Y)=0 \]
Since: \[ P(X \cap Y)=0 \] therefore, events \( X \) and \( Y \) are mutually exclusive.

Final Answer

\[ \boxed{ \text{Events } X \text{ and } Y \text{ are mutually exclusive.} } \] because: \[ P(X \cap Y)=0 \]
