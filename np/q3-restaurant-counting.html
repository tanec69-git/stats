Restaurant Counting Principle App

Problem

After a movie, Andy, Brenda and David decided to go to a restaurant to get some food.
🍲

Soups

5 kinds

🥗

Appetizers

4 kinds

🍛

Main Courses

6 kinds

🥤

Drinks

7 kinds

🍰

Desserts

3 kinds


Andy wants: soup + main course + drink

Brenda wants: (soup + appetizer) OR (dessert + drink)

David wants: either a soup OR drink OR dessert

👦 Andy

🍲 Soup → 5 choices
🍛 Main Course → 6 choices
🥤 Drink → 7 choices

By the Multiplication Principle, multiply the number of choices.

\[ 5 \times 6 \times 7 \]
\[ = 210 \]
Andy has 210 ways

👧 Brenda

Option 1:
🍲 Soup → 5 choices
🥗 Appetizer → 4 choices

OR

Option 2:
🍰 Dessert → 3 choices
🥤 Drink → 7 choices

Use the Multiplication Principle for each option, then use the Addition Principle.

\[ (5 \times 4) + (3 \times 7) \]
\[ = 20 + 21 \]
\[ = 41 \]
Brenda has 41 ways

👦 David

🍲 Soup → 5 choices

OR

🥤 Drink → 7 choices

OR

🍰 Dessert → 3 choices

Since David chooses only ONE item, use the Addition Principle.

\[ 5 + 7 + 3 \]
\[ = 15 \]
David has 15 ways
