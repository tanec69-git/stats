MS Teams Probability App 💬

The Question 📝

Forty-three percent of students in School of ICT receive fewer than five MS Teams messages a day. In a random sample of seven students, what is the probability that the number of students receiving fewer than five MS Teams messages a day is:

  1. exactly three?
  2. at most three?
  3. more than three?

Give your answers correct to 3 decimal places.

Find the mean and standard deviation for the number of students receiving fewer than five MS Teams messages a day in such samples of seven students.

Give your answers correct to 3 significant figures.

The Solutions 📐

This is a Binomial Distribution problem. A student either receives fewer than 5 messages (Success) or they do not (Failure).

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 7 students
  • Probability of success ($p$) = 0.43
  • Probability of failure ($q$) = $1 - 0.43 = 0.57$

Let $X \sim \text{Binomial}(7, 0.43)$. The formula is: $$P(X = r) = {}^{n}C_{r} \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$$

(a) Exactly three students

$$P(X = 3) = {}^{7}C_3 (0.43)^3 (0.57)^4$$ $$P(X = 3) = 35 \times 0.079507 \times 0.10556$$ $$P(X = 3) = 0.29374$$

Answer: 0.294 (to 3 d.p.)

(b) At most three students

We need to find $P(X \le 3)$, which is the sum of probabilities for 0, 1, 2, and 3.

$$P(X = 0) = {}^{7}C_0 (0.43)^0 (0.57)^7 \approx 0.01954$$ $$P(X = 1) = {}^{7}C_1 (0.43)^1 (0.57)^6 \approx 0.10323$$ $$P(X = 2) = {}^{7}C_2 (0.43)^2 (0.57)^5 \approx 0.23364$$ $$P(X = 3) \approx 0.29374 \text{ (from part a)}$$ $$P(X \le 3) = 0.01954 + 0.10323 + 0.23364 + 0.29374 = 0.65015$$

Answer: 0.650 (to 3 d.p.)

(c) More than three students

We need $P(X > 3)$. The most efficient method is using the complement rule: $1 - P(X \le 3)$.

$$P(X > 3) = 1 - P(X \le 3)$$ $$P(X > 3) = 1 - 0.65015 = 0.34985$$

Answer: 0.350 (to 3 d.p.)

Mean and Standard Deviation

Mean ($\mu$):

$$\mu = n \cdot p$$ $$\mu = 7 \times 0.43 = 3.01$$

Answer: Mean = 3.01 (to 3 s.f.)


Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):

$$\sigma = \sqrt{n \cdot p \cdot q}$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{7 \times 0.43 \times 0.57}$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{1.7157} \approx 1.3098$$

Answer: Standard Deviation = 1.31 (to 3 s.f.)
