Surgical Procedure Probability App 🩺

The Question 📝

5. A certain surgical procedure has an 80% chance of success. A doctor performs the procedure on six patients. The random variable represents the number of successful operations.

(a) Show the above fulfils the 4 conditions of a binomial experiment.

(b) Define the random variable, X.
Find the probability for each value of x.
Hence, construct the binomial distribution.

(c) Extend the distribution in (b) by calculating and adding another rows or columns for

  1. cumulative probability P(X≤x).
    Explain in your own words the meaning of P(X≤x) and how the values vary. (Hint: recall cumulative frequency distribution in Ch 3)
  2. probability P(X > x).
    Explain the relationship of P(X > x) and P(X≤x), and how the values vary.

(d) Find the expected number of successful operations for the distribution, and the standard deviation.

(e) Find the probability that the surgery is successful for

  1. more than 3 patients;
  2. at most one.

Give your answers correct to 4 decimal places.

Part (a): 4 Conditions of a Binomial Experiment

  1. Fixed number of trials: There are exactly $n = 6$ operations performed.
  2. Two mutually exclusive outcomes: Each surgery is either a "success" or a "failure".
  3. Constant probability of success: The chance of success is $p = 0.8$ for every single procedure.
  4. Independent trials: The outcome of one patient's surgery does not affect the outcome of another's.

Part (b): Defining X & Constructing the Distribution

Definition: Let $X$ be the random variable representing the number of successful operations out of 6. The possible values are $x = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6$.

Using the formula $P(X = x) = {}^{6}C_{x} (0.8)^x (0.2)^{6-x}$, we calculate the probabilities:

  • $P(X = 0) = {}^{6}C_0 (0.8)^0 (0.2)^6 = 0.000064$
  • $P(X = 1) = {}^{6}C_1 (0.8)^1 (0.2)^5 = 0.001536$
  • $P(X = 2) = {}^{6}C_2 (0.8)^2 (0.2)^4 = 0.015360$
  • $P(X = 3) = {}^{6}C_3 (0.8)^3 (0.2)^3 = 0.081920$
  • $P(X = 4) = {}^{6}C_4 (0.8)^4 (0.2)^2 = 0.245760$
  • $P(X = 5) = {}^{6}C_5 (0.8)^5 (0.2)^1 = 0.393216$
  • $P(X = 6) = {}^{6}C_6 (0.8)^6 (0.2)^0 = 0.262144$

(i) Meaning of $P(X \le x)$: This is the cumulative probability, representing the chance of getting at most $x$ successful operations. Similar to a cumulative frequency curve (ogive), these values are monotonically increasing. They start very small at $x=0$ and accumulate up to exactly $1.0$ at $x=6$.


(ii) Relationship between $P(X > x)$ and $P(X \le x)$: They are complementary events. The relationship is defined by $P(X > x) = 1 - P(X \le x)$. As $x$ increases, $P(X \le x)$ grows larger, forcing $P(X > x)$ to grow smaller, eventually reaching $0$ because you cannot have more than 6 successes in 6 trials.

Part (d): Expected Number and Standard Deviation

Mean (Expected Number, $\mu$):

$$\mu = n \cdot p = 6 \times 0.8 = 4.8$$

Answer: 4.8000


Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):

$$\sigma = \sqrt{n \cdot p \cdot q} = \sqrt{6 \times 0.8 \times 0.2} = \sqrt{0.96} \approx 0.97979$$

Answer: 0.9798 (to 4 d.p.)

Part (e): Probabilities of Specific Outcomes

(i) More than 3 patients: $P(X > 3)$

$$P(X > 3) = P(X=4) + P(X=5) + P(X=6)$$ $$P(X > 3) = 0.24576 + 0.393216 + 0.262144 = 0.90112$$

Answer: 0.9011 (to 4 d.p.)


(ii) At most one patient: $P(X \le 1)$

$$P(X \le 1) = P(X=0) + P(X=1)$$ $$P(X \le 1) = 0.000064 + 0.001536 = 0.0016$$

Answer: 0.0016 (to 4 d.p.)
