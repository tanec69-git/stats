Part (a): Probability in a Sample of 10

This is a Binomial Distribution problem because there are a fixed number of independent trials (customers), each with two outcomes (pays by credit card or doesn't), and a constant probability of success.

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 10
  • Probability of paying by card ($p$) = 0.67
  • Probability of NOT paying by card ($q$) = $1 - 0.67 = 0.33$

Let $X \sim \text{Binomial}(10, 0.67)$. The general formula using ${}^nC_r$ notation is:

$$P(X = r) = {}^{n}C_{r} \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$$

(i) Exactly two customers pay by credit card ✌️

$$P(X = 2) = {}^{10}C_2 (0.67)^2 (0.33)^8$$ $$P(X = 2) = 45 \times 0.4489 \times 0.0001406$$ $$P(X = 2) \approx 0.0028$$

(ii) At least 8 paid by credit card 📈

We need to find $P(X \ge 8)$, which is the sum of probabilities for 8, 9, and 10.

$$P(X = 8) = {}^{10}C_8 (0.67)^8 (0.33)^2 \approx 0.1990$$ $$P(X = 9) = {}^{10}C_9 (0.67)^9 (0.33)^1 \approx 0.0898$$ $$P(X = 10) = {}^{10}C_{10} (0.67)^{10} (0.33)^0 \approx 0.0182$$ $$P(X \ge 8) = 0.1990 + 0.0898 + 0.0182 \approx 0.3070$$

(iii) More than 2 paid by credit card 📊

We need $P(X > 2)$. It is faster to use the complement rule: $1 - P(X \le 2)$.

$$P(X \le 2) = P(X=0) + P(X=1) + P(X=2)$$ $$P(X = 0) = {}^{10}C_0 (0.67)^0 (0.33)^{10} \approx 0.000015$$ $$P(X = 1) = {}^{10}C_1 (0.67)^1 (0.33)^9 \approx 0.000305$$ $$P(X = 2) \approx 0.002841 \text{ (from part i)}$$ $$P(X \le 2) = 0.000015 + 0.000305 + 0.002841 = 0.003161$$ $$P(X > 2) = 1 - 0.003161 \approx 0.9968$$

Part (b): Mean in a Sample of 100 💵

Here, we are looking at the number of customers who do not pay by credit card.

  • Sample size ($n$) = 100
  • Probability of not paying by card ($q$) = 0.33

The formula for the expected value (mean) of a binomial distribution is $\mu = n \cdot p_{event}$.

$$\text{Mean} = 100 \times 0.33 = 33 \text{ customers}$$
