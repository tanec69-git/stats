Light Bulb Quality Control App 💡

The Question 📝

7. In a large consignment of light bulbs, the probability of a bulb being faulty is 0.1. The bulbs are packed in lots of size 10. A quality control officer takes a lot and rejects it if there are at least 2 faulty bulbs. Otherwise, he accepts it.

To confirm his decision, a second quality control officer takes another lot from the batch inspected by the first officer and also rejects the lot if he finds at least 2 faulty bulbs. Otherwise he accepts it.

The whole consignment is accepted if both officers accept their lots and rejected if both officers reject their lots. If they differ in decision, a 3rd officer is brought in to examine a 3rd lot, and the whole consignment is rejected if there are more than 1 faulty bulb.

Find the probability that the whole consignment is finally rejected.

Give your answers correct to 3 significant figures.

1st Officer 2nd Officer 3rd Officer
P(X<2) P(X<2) Accepted
P(X≥2) P(X≥2) Rejected
P(X<2) P(X≥2) P(X<2) Accepted
P(X≥2) P(X<2) P(X≥2) Rejected

Step 1: Probability for a Single Lot 📦

Let $X$ be the number of faulty bulbs in a lot of 10. This follows a binomial distribution: $X \sim \text{Binomial}(10, 0.1)$.

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 10 bulbs
  • Probability of a faulty bulb ($p$) = 0.1
  • Probability of a working bulb ($q$) = 0.9

Accepting a Lot: $P(X < 2)$

The lot is accepted if there are 0 or 1 faulty bulbs.

$$P(X < 2) = P(X=0) + P(X=1)$$ $$P(X = 0) = {}^{10}C_0 (0.1)^0 (0.9)^{10} \approx 0.348678$$ $$P(X = 1) = {}^{10}C_1 (0.1)^1 (0.9)^9 \approx 0.387420$$ $$P(\text{Accept}) = 0.348678 + 0.387420 = \mathbf{0.736098}$$

Rejecting a Lot: $P(X \ge 2)$

The lot is rejected if there are at least 2 faulty bulbs (which is the same mathematically as "more than 1 faulty bulb"). We use the complement rule.

$$P(X \ge 2) = 1 - P(X < 2)$$ $$P(\text{Reject}) = 1 - 0.736098 = \mathbf{0.263902}$$

Step 2: Probability of Consignment Rejection 🚫

The entire consignment is rejected in three specific scenarios (branches on a probability tree):

  1. Reject, Reject: Both the 1st and 2nd officers reject.
  2. Accept, Reject, Reject: 1st accepts, 2nd rejects, 3rd rejects.
  3. Reject, Accept, Reject: 1st rejects, 2nd accepts, 3rd rejects.

Scenario 1: $P(\text{Reject}) \times P(\text{Reject})$

$$0.263902 \times 0.263902 \approx 0.069644$$

Scenario 2: $P(\text{Accept}) \times P(\text{Reject}) \times P(\text{Reject})$

$$0.736098 \times 0.263902 \times 0.263902 \approx 0.051264$$

Scenario 3: $P(\text{Reject}) \times P(\text{Accept}) \times P(\text{Reject})$

$$0.263902 \times 0.736098 \times 0.263902 \approx 0.051264$$

Total Probability:

$$\text{Total} = 0.069644 + 0.051264 + 0.051264 = 0.172172$$

Final Answer: 0.172 (to 3 s.f.)
