Campus T-Shirt Shop App 👕

The Question 📝

4. It was observed that the probability that a student who visits the Campus T-shirt shop, will purchase a T-shirt is 0.3.

(a) If 8 students visit the shop,

  1. what is the probability that 5 of them will make a purchase?
  2. what is the probability that less than 3 will make a purchase?

(b) On a particular day, 10 groups of 8 students will be visiting the shop, what is the probability that 2 of the groups would purchase 5 T-shirts each?

Part (a) Solutions 🛍️

This is a Binomial Distribution. A student either purchases a T-shirt (Success) or does not (Failure).

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 8 students
  • Probability of success ($p$) = 0.3
  • Probability of failure ($q$) = $1 - 0.3 = 0.7$

Let $X \sim \text{Binomial}(8, 0.3)$. The formula is: $$P(X = r) = {}^{n}C_{r} \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$$

(i) Exactly 5 students make a purchase

$$P(X = 5) = {}^{8}C_5 (0.3)^5 (0.7)^3$$ $$P(X = 5) = 56 \times 0.00243 \times 0.343$$ $$P(X = 5) = 0.046675$$

Answer: 0.0467 (to 4 s.f.)

(ii) Less than 3 make a purchase

We need to find $P(X < 3)$, which means finding the probabilities for 0, 1, and 2.

$$P(X < 3) = P(X=0) + P(X=1) + P(X=2)$$ $$P(X = 0) = {}^{8}C_0 (0.3)^0 (0.7)^8 \approx 0.057648$$ $$P(X = 1) = {}^{8}C_1 (0.3)^1 (0.7)^7 \approx 0.197650$$ $$P(X = 2) = {}^{8}C_2 (0.3)^2 (0.7)^6 \approx 0.296475$$ $$P(X < 3) = 0.057648 + 0.197650 + 0.296475 = 0.551773$$

Answer: 0.5518 (to 4 s.f.)

Part (b) Solutions 🏫 (Nested Binomial)

This is a secondary binomial distribution. Let $Y$ be the number of groups (out of 10) that purchase exactly 5 T-shirts.

  • New trials ($n'$) = 10 groups
  • New probability of success ($p'$) = $P(X=5) \text{ from part a(i)} = 0.046675$
  • New probability of failure ($q'$) = $1 - 0.046675 = 0.953325$

We want to find the probability that exactly 2 groups do this, $P(Y = 2)$:

$$P(Y = 2) = {}^{10}C_2 (0.046675)^2 (0.953325)^8$$ $$P(Y = 2) = 45 \times 0.0021786 \times 0.6823467$$ $$P(Y = 2) = 0.066870$$

Answer: 0.0669 (to 4 s.f.)
