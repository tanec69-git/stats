Polytechnic Probability App ✍️

The Question 📝

3. In a polytechnic, 1 student in 5 is left-handed.

(a) Find the probability that in a class of 10 students

  1. exactly 3 will be left-handed,
  2. more than half will be left-handed.

(b) Find the mean and the standard deviation of the number of left-handed people in a group of 25 students.

(c) In 8 classes, each with 10 students, what is the probability of finding 2 classes with exactly 3 left-handed students?

Give your answers correct to 4 decimal places.

Part (a) Solutions 📐

This is a Binomial Distribution. A student is either left-handed (Success) or right-handed/ambidextrous (Failure).

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 10 students
  • Probability of success ($p$) = $\frac{1}{5} = 0.2$
  • Probability of failure ($q$) = $1 - 0.2 = 0.8$

Let $X \sim \text{Binomial}(10, 0.2)$. The formula is: $$P(X = r) = {}^{n}C_{r} \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$$

(i) Exactly 3 left-handed students

$$P(X = 3) = {}^{10}C_3 (0.2)^3 (0.8)^7$$ $$P(X = 3) = 120 \times 0.008 \times 0.209715$$ $$P(X = 3) = 0.201326$$

Answer: 0.2013

(ii) More than half are left-handed

More than half of 10 is strictly greater than 5. We need to find $P(X > 5)$, which is $P(X \ge 6)$.

$$P(X > 5) = P(X=6) + P(X=7) + P(X=8) + P(X=9) + P(X=10)$$ $$P(X = 6) = {}^{10}C_6 (0.2)^6 (0.8)^4 \approx 0.005505$$ $$P(X = 7) = {}^{10}C_7 (0.2)^7 (0.8)^3 \approx 0.000786$$ $$P(X = 8) = {}^{10}C_8 (0.2)^8 (0.8)^2 \approx 0.000074$$ $$P(X = 9) = {}^{10}C_9 (0.2)^9 (0.8)^1 \approx 0.000004$$ $$P(X = 10) = {}^{10}C_{10} (0.2)^{10} (0.8)^0 \approx 0.000000$$ $$P(X > 5) = 0.005505 + 0.000786 + 0.000074 + 0.000004 + 0 = 0.006369$$

Answer: 0.0064

Part (b) Solutions 📊

Here we look at a larger group where $n = 25$ and $p = 0.2$.

Mean ($\mu$):

$$\mu = n \cdot p = 25 \times 0.2 = 5$$

Answer: Mean = 5.0000


Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):

$$\sigma = \sqrt{n \cdot p \cdot q} = \sqrt{25 \times 0.2 \times 0.8}$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{4} = 2$$

Answer: Standard Deviation = 2.0000

Part (c) Solutions 🧩 (Nested Binomial)

This is a secondary binomial distribution. Let $Y$ be the number of classes (out of 8) that have exactly 3 left-handed students.

  • New trials ($n'$) = 8 classes
  • New probability of success ($p'$) = $P(X=3) \text{ from part (a)} = 0.201326$
  • New probability of failure ($q'$) = $1 - 0.201326 = 0.798674$

We want to find $P(Y = 2)$:

$$P(Y = 2) = {}^{8}C_2 (0.201326)^2 (0.798674)^6$$ $$P(Y = 2) = 28 \times 0.040532 \times 0.259443$$ $$P(Y = 2) = 0.294435$$

Answer: 0.2944
