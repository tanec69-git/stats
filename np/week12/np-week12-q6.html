The Question 📝

6. A biologist did a study on 20 seeds of a particular species of plant and found that the probability of its seeds germinating successfully is 0.75. The germination of each seed is independent of the others.

  1. Calculate the expected number of seeds that germinate successfully and the value of its standard deviation.
  2. Calculate the probability that at least 16 seeds germinate successfully.

Part (a): Expected Number and Standard Deviation 🧮

This is a Binomial Distribution problem because we have a fixed number of independent trials (seeds), each with two outcomes (germinates or doesn't), and a constant probability of success.

  • Number of trials ($n$) = 20 seeds
  • Probability of success ($p$) = 0.75
  • Probability of failure ($q$) = $1 - 0.75 = 0.25$

Expected Number (Mean, $\mu$):

$$\mu = n \cdot p$$ $$\mu = 20 \times 0.75 = 15$$

Answer: 15 seeds

Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):

$$\sigma = \sqrt{n \cdot p \cdot q}$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{20 \times 0.75 \times 0.25}$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{3.75} \approx 1.93649$$

Answer: 1.936 (to 4 s.f.)

Part (b): Probability of At Least 16 Seeds 🌼

Let $X \sim \text{Binomial}(20, 0.75)$. We need to find the probability that at least 16 seeds germinate, which is $P(X \ge 16)$.

This is the sum of the probabilities of exactly 16, 17, 18, 19, and 20 seeds germinating. We use the formula $P(X = r) = {}^{n}C_{r} \cdot p^r \cdot q^{n-r}$.

$$P(X \ge 16) = P(X=16) + P(X=17) + P(X=18) + P(X=19) + P(X=20)$$

Calculating each term:

$$P(X = 16) = {}^{20}C_{16} (0.75)^{16} (0.25)^4 \approx 0.189686$$ $$P(X = 17) = {}^{20}C_{17} (0.75)^{17} (0.25)^3 \approx 0.133896$$ $$P(X = 18) = {}^{20}C_{18} (0.75)^{18} (0.25)^2 \approx 0.066948$$ $$P(X = 19) = {}^{20}C_{19} (0.75)^{19} (0.25)^1 \approx 0.021141$$ $$P(X = 20) = {}^{20}C_{20} (0.75)^{20} (0.25)^0 \approx 0.003171$$

Summing them up:

$$P(X \ge 16) = 0.189686 + 0.133896 + 0.066948 + 0.021141 + 0.003171 = 0.414842$$

Answer: 0.4148 (to 4 d.p.)
