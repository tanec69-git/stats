🔠🔢 Staff ID Number Arrangements

Learn counting principles using company ID numbers!
A company assigns its staff ID numbers using a sequence of:

• 2 uppercase letters 🔠
• followed by 4 digits 🔢
The letters must be distinct, but the digits can be repeated.

How many different ID numbers are possible?

🔤 Step 1: Arrange the Letters

The first letter can be any of the 26 uppercase letters.
First Letter = 26 choices

The second letter must be different.
Second Letter = 25 choices

Total ways for the letters: \[ 26 \times 25 \] \[ = 650 \]

🔢 Step 2: Arrange the Digits

Each digit can be: \[ 0 \text{ to } 9 \] So each digit has: \[ 10 \text{ choices} \] Since repetition is allowed: \[ 10 \times 10 \times 10 \times 10 \] \[ = 10^4 \] \[ = 10000 \]

🧮 Step 3: Multiply the Choices

Total number of ID numbers: \[ 26 \times 25 \times 10^4 \] \[ = 650 \times 10000 \] \[ = 6,500,000 \]
✅ Final Answer: \[ \boxed{6,500,000} \]
