TransitBus Fare Function

A bus company, TransitBus, charges passengers based on the number of stops traveled.

First 3 stops (inclusive): Flat fee of $1.50
Each additional stop beyond 3 stops: $0.50 per stop
Maximum fare: $5.00 for any journey

Let \(x\) represent the number of stops traveled (\(x\) is a positive integer).

(i) Express the total fare (in dollars), \(C(x)\), as a function of \(x\).
(ii) Calculate the total fare for a journey completed in:

(I) 2 stops
(II) 6 stops
(III) 10 stops
(iii) Determine whether \(C(x)\) is a one-to-one function or a many-to-one function. Explain your answer.
(iv) Find the total fare for a journey of 8 stops.
(v) State the maximum fare charged by TransitBus.
(i)
\[ C(x)= \begin{cases} 1.50 & \text{if } 1 \le x \le 3, \\\\ 1.50+0.50(x-3) & \text{if } 4 \le x \le 9, \\\\ 5.00 & \text{if } x \ge 10 \end{cases} \]
(ii)

(I) 2 stops

\[ C(2)=\$1.50 \]

(II) 6 stops

\[ C(6)=1.50+0.50(6-3) \] \[ =1.50+1.50 \] \[ =\$3.00 \]

(III) 10 stops

\[ C(10)=\$5.00 \]
(iii)
It is a many-to-one function.

This is because the total fare remains the same after the number of stops exceeds 9.
(iv) \[ C(8)=1.50+0.50(8-3) \] \[ =1.50+2.50 \] \[ =\$4.00 \]
(v)
\[ \text{Maximum fare}=\$5.00 \]
