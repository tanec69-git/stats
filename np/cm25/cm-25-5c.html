🎾⚽ Probability Using Venn Diagrams

Find the probability of a student choosing both games!
30 students from a class can choose to play 🎾 Tennis or ⚽ Football.

• 5 choose Tennis only
• 7 choose Football only
• 3 choose no games

If a student is selected at random, what is the probability of the student choosing to play both games?

📘 Step 1: Find students who play both games

Total students: \[ 30 \] Students already accounted for: \[ 5 + 7 + 3 = 15 \] Therefore, students who play both games: \[ 30 - 15 = 15 \]

🎨 Venn Diagram Representation

🧮 Mathematical Calculation Method

Probability means: \[ \text{Probability} = \frac{\text{Number of favourable outcomes}} {\text{Total number of outcomes}} \] Favourable outcomes: students who play both games \[ = 15 \] Total students: \[ = 30 \] Therefore, \[ P(\text{both games}) = \frac{15}{30} \] Simplify: \[ = \frac{1}{2} \]
✅ Final Answer: \[ \boxed{ \frac{1}{2} } \]
