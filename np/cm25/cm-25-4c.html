✈️ Travel Itinerary Permutations 🌍

Learn permutations with travel routes and restrictions!
A tourist plans to visit 5 cities:
London, Paris, Milan, Brussels and Amsterdam

Each city can be visited only once.

How many valid travel itineraries are possible if the trip must:

(i) start in London and end in Amsterdam.

(ii) start and end in London. Only London can be visited twice.

(iii) in addition to part (ii), Paris and Milan must be visited on consecutive days.

📍 (i) Start in London and End in Amsterdam

London and Amsterdam are fixed at the beginning and end.
London → ? → ? → ? → Amsterdam

Remaining cities to arrange:
Paris
Milan
Brussels

Number of arrangements: \[ 3! \] \[ = 6 \]
✅ Answer: \[ \boxed{6} \]

🏙️ (ii) Start and End in London

London appears at both the start and end.
London → ? → ? → ? → ? → London

Arrange the 4 middle cities:
Paris
Milan
Brussels
Amsterdam

Number of arrangements: \[ 4! \] \[ = 24 \]
✅ Answer: \[ \boxed{24} \]

🔗 (iii) Paris and Milan Consecutive

Paris and Milan must stay together.

Treat them as ONE block.

Possible internal arrangements:
Paris → Milan
Milan → Paris


We now arrange:
• (Paris-Milan block)
• Brussels
• Amsterdam

Number of ways to arrange these 3 objects: \[ 3! \] \[ = 6 \]
Number of ways within the block: \[ 2! \] \[ = 2 \]
Total number of itineraries: \[ 3! \times 2! \] \[ = 6 \times 2 \] \[ = 12 \]
✅ Answer: \[ \boxed{12} \]
