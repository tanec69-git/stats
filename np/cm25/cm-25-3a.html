📊 Frequency Table Statistics

Mean, Mode, Population Variance and Standard Deviation
Data from 50 students are collected from a school to determine the duration of lunchtime spent in a typical school day.

The table below shows the survey results.
Number of minutes spent per student (x) 20 25 30 35 40 45
Frequency (f) 5 8 10 15 7 5
Table 3(a): Frequency Table

Compute:

(i) the mean and mode

(ii) the population variance

(iii) the standard deviation

📋 Completed Working Table

x f fx x² fx²
20 5 100 400 2000
25 8 200 625 5000
30 10 300 900 9000
35 15 525 1225 18375
40 7 280 1600 11200
45 5 225 2025 10125
Total 50 1630 6775 55700

📘 Mean

\[ \mu = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f} \] \[ = \frac{1630}{50} \] \[ = 32.6 \]
✅ Mean: \[ \boxed{32.6 \text{ minutes}} \]

📙 Mode

Highest frequency: \[ 15 \] corresponding to: \[ x = 35 \]
✅ Mode: \[ \boxed{35 \text{ minutes}} \]

📗 Population Variance

\[ \sigma^2 = \frac{\sum fx^2}{\sum f} - \mu^2 \] \[ = \frac{55700}{50} - 32.6^2 \] \[ = 1114 - 1062.76 \] \[ = \frac{1281}{25} \text{ or } 51.24 \]
✅ Population Variance: \[ \boxed{51.24} \]

📕 Standard Deviation

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2} \] \[ = \sqrt{51.24} \] \[ = 7.16 \]
✅ Standard Deviation: \[ \boxed{7.16 \text{ minutes}} \]
