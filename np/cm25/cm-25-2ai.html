Finding an Inverse Function

\[ \text{Find } g^{-1}(x) \text{ if } \quad g(x)=\sqrt{2x+4}, \quad x \geq -2,\; x \in \mathbb{R} \]
Step 1: Let \[ y=\sqrt{2x+4} \]
Step 2: Square both sides \[ y^2=2x+4 \]
Step 3: Make \(x\) the subject \[ y^2-4=2x \] \[ x=\frac{y^2-4}{2} \]
Step 4: Hence, \[ g^{-1}(x)=\frac{x^2-4}{2} \]
Final Answer: \[ g^{-1}(x)=\frac{x^2-4}{2} \]
