Probability Question

Given:
\[ P(A)=0.16 \]
\[ P(D)=0.18 \]
\[ P(D|A)=0.65 \]
where:
  • \(A\): Person is aged 50 years or more
  • \(D\): Person has diabetes
(a) Find the probability that the chosen person is less than 50 years old and has diabetes.

Step 1: Find \(P(A \cap D)\)

\[ P(A \cap D)=P(D|A)\times P(A) \]
\[ =0.65 \times 0.16 \]
\[ =0.104 \]

Step 2: Find the probability that the person is less than 50 and has diabetes.

\[ P(A' \cap D)=P(D)-P(A \cap D) \]
\[ =0.18-0.104 \]
\[ =0.076 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.076} \]
(b) Find the probability that the chosen person is aged 50 years or more, given that the person does not have diabetes.

Step 1: Find \(P(D')\)

\[ P(D')=1-P(D) \]
\[ =1-0.18 \]
\[ =0.82 \]

Step 2: Find \(P(A \cap D')\)

\[ P(A \cap D')=P(A)-P(A \cap D) \]
\[ =0.16-0.104 \]
\[ =0.056 \]

Step 3: Apply conditional probability.

\[ P(A|D')=\frac{P(A \cap D')}{P(D')} \]
\[ =\frac{0.056}{0.82} \]
\[ \approx 0.0683 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.0683} \]
(c) Find the probability that the chosen person is either aged 50 years or more, or has diabetes or both.

Use the addition rule:

\[ P(A \cup D)=P(A)+P(D)-P(A \cap D) \]
\[ =0.16+0.18-0.104 \]
\[ =0.236 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.236} \]
(d) Are \(A\) and \(D\) independent? Explain.

Two events are independent if:

\[ P(D|A)=P(D) \]

Compare the values:

\[ P(D|A)=0.65 \]
\[ P(D)=0.18 \]

Since:

\[ 0.65 \ne 0.18 \]

The events are not independent.

Answer:

\[ \boxed{\text{A and D are not independent}} \]
