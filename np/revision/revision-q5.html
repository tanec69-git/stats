Solutions for Question 5

Population set $X = \{ 83, 85, 86, 90, 91, 93 \}$

Part (a): Mean, Median, and Standard Deviation

1. Show that the mean is 88:

The population mean $\mu$ is the sum of all values divided by the number of values ($N = 6$).

$$ \mu = \frac{83 + 85 + 86 + 90 + 91 + 93}{6} = \frac{528}{6} = 88 $$

2. Find the median:

The set is already arranged in ascending order. Since there are an even number of values ($6$), the median is the average of the two middle values ($3$rd and $4$th values: $86$ and $90$).

$$ \text{Median} = \frac{86 + 90}{2} = \frac{176}{2} = 88 $$

3. Find the standard deviation:

Since this is a population set, we use the population variance formula $\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x - \mu)^2}{N}$. First, let's find the squared deviations from the mean (88):

  • $(83 - 88)^2 = (-5)^2 = 25$
  • $(85 - 88)^2 = (-3)^2 = 9$
  • $(86 - 88)^2 = (-2)^2 = 4$
  • $(90 - 88)^2 = (2)^2 = 4$
  • $(91 - 88)^2 = (3)^2 = 9$
  • $(93 - 88)^2 = (5)^2 = 25$

Sum of squared deviations $= 25 + 9 + 4 + 4 + 9 + 25 = 76$

$$ \text{Variance } (\sigma^2) = \frac{76}{6} = \frac{38}{3} \approx 12.67 $$

$$ \text{Standard Deviation } (\sigma) = \sqrt{\frac{38}{3}} \approx 3.56 \text{ (to 3 s.f.)} $$

Part (b): Moderated Results (Without using summation formulae)

When applying a linear transformation $Y = aX + b$ to a dataset, the new statistics follow these rules:

  • $\text{New Mean } (\mu_Y) = a(\mu_X) + b$
  • $\text{New Variance } (\sigma_Y^2) = a^2(\sigma_X^2)$
  • $\text{New Standard Deviation } (\sigma_Y) = |a|(\sigma_X)$
Option A: Add 3 marks ($Y = X + 3$)

Here, $a = 1$ and $b = 3$. Adding a constant shifts the mean but does not change the spread of the data.

  • New Mean: $88 + 3 = 91$
  • New Variance: remains unchanged at $\frac{38}{3} \approx 12.67$
  • New SD: remains unchanged at $\sqrt{\frac{38}{3}} \approx 3.56$
Option B: Increase each mark by 3% ($Y = 1.03X$)

Here, $a = 1.03$ and $b = 0$. Multiplying by a constant scales all statistics.

  • New Mean: $1.03 \times 88 = 90.64$
  • New Variance: $1.03^2 \times \frac{38}{3} \approx 1.0609 \times 12.667 \approx 13.44$
  • New SD: $1.03 \times \sqrt{\frac{38}{3}} \approx 1.03 \times 3.559 \approx 3.67$

Part (c): Creating a Custom Dataset

Goal: Find a new set $W$ where the mean remains exactly $88$, but the standard deviation is increased by 5% ($\sigma_W = 1.05\sigma_X$).

Reasoning: To stretch the standard deviation by a factor of $1.05$ without moving the center (mean), we must stretch the distance of each individual mark from the mean by 1.05. We can write this relationship as:

$$ w_i - \mu = 1.05(x_i - \mu) $$

Since $\mu = 88$:

$$ w_i - 88 = 1.05(x_i - 88) $$

$$ w_i = 1.05(x_i - 88) + 88 $$

Now, we apply this formula to each of the original 6 marks in set $X$:

Original Mark ($x_i$) Deviation ($x_i - 88$) New Deviation $1.05(x_i - 88)$ New Mark ($w_i$)
83 -5 -5.25 88 - 5.25 = 82.75
85 -3 -3.15 88 - 3.15 = 84.85
86 -2 -2.10 88 - 2.10 = 85.9
90 2 2.10 88 + 2.10 = 90.1
91 3 3.15 88 + 3.15 = 91.15

The 6 marks are: {82.75, 84.85, 85.9, 90.1, 91.15, 93.25}
