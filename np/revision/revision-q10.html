🎲 Probability Solutions

10. A box contains 10 identical coloured balls. There are 5 red balls, 3 yellow balls and 2 blue balls. If 3 coloured balls are randomly selected from the box, what is the probability that:

(a) all the three of them are red in colour?
(b) 2 red balls and 1 yellow ball are selected?
(c) exactly 2 red balls or exactly 2 yellow balls are selected?

[Note: Unless specially specified, the order of selection does not matter.]
Preliminary Calculation: Total Possible Outcomes
Since the order of selection does not matter, we use combinations ($^{n}C_{r}$).
We are selecting $r=3$ balls from a total pool of $n=10$ balls.
$$ \text{Total ways to select 3 balls} = ^{10}C_{3} = \frac{10 \times 9 \times 8}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 120 $$
We will use this $120$ as our denominator for all the probability calculations.

🔴 Part (a): All three are red

To get 3 red balls, we must select all 3 from the pool of 5 available red balls.

$$ \text{Ways to choose 3 red balls} = ^{5}C_{3} = 10 $$ $$ P(\text{3 Red}) = \frac{^{5}C_{3}}{^{10}C_{3}} = \frac{10}{120} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{12}} $$

Answer: 1 / 12

🔴🟡 Part (b): 2 red balls and 1 yellow ball

We need to select 2 balls from the 5 red balls AND 1 ball from the 3 yellow balls. We multiply the combinations together.

$$ \text{Ways} = (^{5}C_{2}) \times (^{3}C_{1}) = 10 \times 3 = 30 $$ $$ P(\text{2 Red, 1 Yellow}) = \frac{^{5}C_{2} \times ^{3}C_{1}}{^{10}C_{3}} = \frac{30}{120} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{4}} $$

Answer: 1 / 4

⚖️ Part (c): Exactly 2 red OR exactly 2 yellow

This asks for the union of two mutually exclusive events (you cannot draw exactly 2 red AND exactly 2 yellow if you are only drawing 3 balls total). We calculate the ways for each scenario separately and add them.

  • Scenario 1: Exactly 2 Red Balls
    Choose 2 from the 5 red balls, and 1 from the remaining 5 non-red balls (3 yellow + 2 blue).
    $$ \text{Ways} = (^{5}C_{2}) \times (^{5}C_{1}) = 10 \times 5 = 50 $$
  • Scenario 2: Exactly 2 Yellow Balls
    Choose 2 from the 3 yellow balls, and 1 from the remaining 7 non-yellow balls (5 red + 2 blue).
    $$ \text{Ways} = (^{3}C_{2}) \times (^{7}C_{1}) = 3 \times 7 = 21 $$

Now, we add the successful outcomes and divide by the total possible outcomes:

$$ P(\text{Exactly 2 Red OR Exactly 2 Yellow}) = \frac{50 + 21}{120} = \mathbf{\frac{71}{120}} $$

Answer: 71 / 120
