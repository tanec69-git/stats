Part (b): Mean and Standard Deviation (in S$)

First, we calculate the statistical measures in thousands of S$, and then multiply by 1,000 to find the exact values in S$.

1. Mean ($\bar{x}$)

$$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f} = \frac{2164}{220} \approx 9.83636 \text{ (in thousands)}$$
Mean in S$: $ 9.83636 \times 1000 = \mathbf{\$9,836.36} $

2. Standard Deviation ($\sigma$)

$$\sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{\sum fx^2}{\sum f} - \left(\frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}\right)^2 }$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{28560}{220} - \left(\frac{2164}{220}\right)^2 }$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{ 129.81818 - 96.75405 }$$ $$\sigma = \sqrt{ 33.06413 } \approx 5.75014 \text{ (in thousands)}$$
Standard Deviation in S$: $ 5.75014 \times 1000 = \mathbf{\$5,750.14} $

Part (c): Evaluating Options A & B Without Further Calculation

Statistical Rules for Linear Transformations:
If a dataset $X$ is transformed by the equation $Y = aX + b$:
1. New Mean = $a(\text{Old Mean}) + b$
2. New Standard Deviation = $|a| \times (\text{Old Standard Deviation})$
Note: Adding a constant ($b$) shifts the data but does not change the spread (standard deviation).

(i) Option A: Increase every bonus by 25%

Increasing the bonus by 25% means multiplying the original payouts by $1.25$ ($Y = 1.25X$).

  • New Mean: $ 1.25 \times \$9,836.36 = \mathbf{\$12,295.45} $
  • New Standard Deviation: $ 1.25 \times \$5,750.14 = \mathbf{\$7,187.68} $

(ii) Option B: Pay every employee S$500 in addition

Adding a flat S$500 means adding a constant to the original payouts ($Y = X + 500$).

  • New Mean: $ \$9,836.36 + \$500 = \mathbf{\$10,336.36} $
  • New Standard Deviation: Remains unchanged at $\mathbf{\$5,750.14}$ (a constant addition does not affect data dispersion).
