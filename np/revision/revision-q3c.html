Part (c)(i)

Problem: Find the largest possible domain of the function \( f(x) = \frac{7}{x - 7} \).

Step-by-step Solution:

For a rational function to be defined, its denominator cannot be equal to zero. Therefore, we set up the inequality:

$$ x - 7 \neq 0 $$

$$ x \neq 7 $$

The domain is all real numbers except 7.

Set-builder notation:

The domain of f(x) is $$\{ x:x \neq 7, x \in \mathbb{R} \} $$

Part (c)(ii)

Problem: Find the range of the function \( g(x) = 62 - 2x \) for \( 5 \le x < 9, x \in \mathbb{R} \).

Step-by-step Solution:

To find the range rigorously, we can algebraically manipulate the domain inequality to match the function \( g(x) \).

1. Start with the given domain:

$$ 5 \le x < 9 $$

2. Multiply the entire inequality by \(-2\) (Remember that multiplying by a negative number flips the inequality signs):

$$ -10 \ge -2x > -18 $$

3. Add \(62\) to all parts of the inequality to form \( g(x) = 62 - 2x \):

$$ 62 - 10 \ge 62 - 2x > 62 - 18 $$

$$ 52 \ge g(x) > 44 $$

4. Rewrite it in standard ascending order:

$$ 44 < g(x) \le 52 $$

Set-builder notation:

$$ \{ g(x):44 < g(x) \le 52, g(x) \in \mathbb{R} \} $$
