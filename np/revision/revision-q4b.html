Solutions for Question 4(b)

Part (b)(i)

Problem: Find \( f^{-1}(x) \) for \( f(x) = 2x + 3 \).

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Replace \( f(x) \) with \( y \).

$$ y = 2x + 3 $$

Step 2: Swap \( x \) and \( y \) to find the inverse.

$$ x = 2y + 3 $$

Step 3: Solve for \( y \).

Subtract 3 from both sides:

$$ x - 3 = 2y $$

Divide by 2:

$$ y = \frac{x - 3}{2} $$

Step 4: Replace \( y \) with \( f^{-1}(x) \).

$$ f^{-1}(x) = \frac{x - 3}{2} $$

Part (b)(ii)

Problem: Find \( f^{-1}(x) \) for \( f(x) = \frac{3x + 1}{2 - x} \), \( x \neq 2 \).

Step-by-step Solution:

Step 1: Replace \( f(x) \) with \( y \).

$$ y = \frac{3x + 1}{2 - x} $$

Step 2: Swap \( x \) and \( y \).

$$ x = \frac{3y + 1}{2 - y} $$

Step 3: Solve for \( y \).

Multiply both sides by the denominator \( (2 - y) \) to remove the fraction:

$$ x(2 - y) = 3y + 1 $$

Distribute the \( x \):

$$ 2x - xy = 3y + 1 $$

Group all terms containing \( y \) on one side of the equation, and everything else on the other side. Let's move \( -xy \) to the right and \( 1 \) to the left:

$$ 2x - 1 = 3y + xy $$

Factor out \( y \) on the right side:

$$ 2x - 1 = y(3 + x) $$

Divide by \( (3 + x) \) to isolate \( y \):

$$ y = \frac{2x - 1}{x + 3} $$

Step 4: Replace \( y \) with \( f^{-1}(x) \) and note the new restriction.

Since the denominator cannot be zero, \( x + 3 \neq 0 \implies x \neq -3 \).

$$ f^{-1}(x) = \frac{2x - 1}{x + 3}, \quad x \neq -3 $$
