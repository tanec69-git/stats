Expressway Speed Statistics

6. The table below gives the frequency distribution of the speeds (in km/h) of 150 cars travelling on the outermost lane of a stretch of an expressway, as surveyed by the Traffic Police Department.

Speed (km/h) 60708090100110120
Frequency 918273641109

From the above table,
(a) find the mean, mode, median, standard deviation, of the speeds.
(b) Comment on the skewness of the distribution.

Part (a): Statistical Measures

1. Mean (\(\bar{x}\))

$$ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f} = \frac{13480}{150} $$ $$ \bar{x} \approx 89.87 \text{ km/h} $$

2. Mode

The mode corresponds to the data value with the highest frequency. The highest frequency is 41, which occurs at a speed of 100 km/h.

$$ \text{Mode} = 100 \text{ km/h} $$

3. Median

With a total frequency of \(n = 150\), the median position is the average of the 75th and 76th values. Calculating cumulative frequencies:

  • \(\le 60\) km/h: 9 cars
  • \(\le 70\) km/h: 9 + 18 = 27 cars
  • \(\le 80\) km/h: 27 + 27 = 54 cars
  • \(\le 90\) km/h: 54 + 36 = 90 cars

Both the 75th and 76th values fall within the 90 km/h group.

$$ \text{Median} = 90 \text{ km/h} $$

4. Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\))

Using the standard deviation formula for grouped data:

$$ \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{\sum fx^2}{\sum f} - \left(\frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}\right)^2 } $$ $$ \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{1245600}{150} - \left(\frac{13480}{150}\right)^2 } $$ $$ \sigma = \sqrt{ 8304 - (89.8667)^2 } $$ $$ \sigma = \sqrt{ 8304 - 8076.0178 } $$ $$ \sigma = \sqrt{ 227.9822 } \approx 15.10 \text{ km/h} $$

Part (b): Distribution Skewness

To evaluate skewness, we compare the relative positions of the Mean, Median, and Mode.

  • Mean: 89.87 km/h
  • Median: 90.00 km/h
  • Mode: 100.00 km/h
Observation: Mean < Median < Mode (89.87 < 90 < 100)

Conclusion: Because the mean is less than the median and the mode, the tail of the distribution extends towards the lower values. Therefore, the distribution is negatively skewed (skewed to the left).
