📝 Combinatorics Problem Solutions

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Part (a): Committee Selection

Question: There are 32 Chinese, 9 Malays and 4 Indians in a class. How many ways can a committee of four be selected, if it must comprise of 2 Chinese, a Malay and an Indian?

Since the order of selection does not matter for a committee, we use combinations ($^{n}C_{r}$).

  • Select 2 Chinese from 32: $^{32}C_{2}$
  • Select 1 Malay from 9: $^{9}C_{1}$
  • Select 1 Indian from 4: $^{4}C_{1}$
$$ \text{Total ways} = ^{32}C_{2} \times ^{9}C_{1} \times ^{4}C_{1} $$ $$ \text{Total ways} = 496 \times 9 \times 4 = \mathbf{17,856} $$

Answer: 17,856 ways

📚 Part (b): Book Selection

Question: On a particular rack of a book shelf, there are four English books, two Chinese books, three Malay books and 5 Tamil books. In how many ways can a person choose five books if:
  1. there is no restriction;
  2. there must be exactly three English books;
  3. there must be exactly two English books and one Malay book;
  4. there must not be any English books;
  5. there must be at least one Chinese book; (Note: Labeled as 'iii' again in original text)

Total Pool: 4 English (E) + 2 Chinese (C) + 3 Malay (M) + 5 Tamil (T) = 14 books.

(i) No restriction

Choose any 5 books from the total 14.

$$ ^{14}C_{5} = \mathbf{2,002} $$

(ii) Exactly three English books

Choose 3 from the 4 English books, and the remaining 2 from the 10 non-English books.

$$ ^{4}C_{3} \times ^{10}C_{2} = 4 \times 45 = \mathbf{180} $$

(iii) Exactly two English and one Malay book

We need 5 books total. Choose 2 English, 1 Malay, and the remaining 2 from the 7 other books (Chinese + Tamil).

$$ ^{4}C_{2} \times ^{3}C_{1} \times ^{7}C_{2} = 6 \times 3 \times 21 = \mathbf{378} $$

(iv) No English books

Choose all 5 books from the 10 non-English books.

$$ ^{10}C_{5} = \mathbf{252} $$

(v) At least one Chinese book

We can use the complement method: Total unrestricted ways minus the ways with zero Chinese books.

$$ \text{Total ways} - \text{Ways with no Chinese books} $$ $$ ^{14}C_{5} - ^{12}C_{5} = 2002 - 792 = \mathbf{1,210} $$

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Part (c): Concert Seating Arrangements

Question: If there are 2 families, each consist of father, mother, a son and a daughter. They go to watch a concert together. If they are sitting in the same row with 8 seats, how many ways can they be seated if:
  1. there is no restriction;
  2. all the males must sit together;
  3. all the males and all the females must sit together;
  4. the sons must not sit together?

Total People: 8 (4 Males: 2 Fathers, 2 Sons | 4 Females: 2 Mothers, 2 Daughters)

(i) No restriction

Arrange 8 people in 8 seats. Order matters here, so we use factorials ($n!$).

$$ 8! = \mathbf{40,320} $$

(ii) All the males must sit together

Treat the 4 males as 1 single block. We now arrange 5 units (4 individual females + 1 male block). Finally, arrange the 4 males within their block.

$$ 5! \times 4! = 120 \times 24 = \mathbf{2,880} $$

(iii) All males together AND all females together

Treat males as 1 block and females as 1 block. Arrange the 2 blocks, arrange the males within their block, and arrange the females within their block.

$$ 2! \times 4! \times 4! = 2 \times 24 \times 24 = \mathbf{1,152} $$

(iv) The sons must not sit together

Method 1: Complement
Find total ways minus the ways where the 2 sons sit together. If sons are a block, there are 7 units to arrange.

$$ 8! - (7! \times 2!) = 40320 - (5040 \times 2) = 40320 - 10080 = \mathbf{30,240} $$

Method 2: Slotting (Insertion Method)
Arrange the 6 other family members first ($6!$). This creates 7 potential slots (including ends) for the 2 sons to sit separately ($^{7}P_{2}$).

$$ 6! \times ^{7}P_{2} = 720 \times (7 \times 6) = 720 \times 42 = \mathbf{30,240} $$
