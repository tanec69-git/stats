🏊‍♂️🎾⚽ Sports Venn Diagram Solutions

1. (d) 600 students were asked to state which of the following sports they liked: swimming (S), tennis (T), and football (F).

200 said swimming,
250 said tennis, and
300 said football.
60 said they liked swimming and football, but not tennis.
40 said like all three.
55 liked swimming and tennis; and
35 liked tennis and football but not swimming.

(i) Complete a Venn diagram showing the actual numbers in each of the components.
(ii) How many students like at least one of the sports?
(iii) How many students like none of the sports?
(iv) Find the number of students who like only one sport.
(v) Find the number of students who like Tennis but not Football.
🔍 Step-by-Step Regional Deductions
Before answering the specific parts, let's calculate the exact number of students in each of the 8 distinct, non-overlapping regions of the Venn diagram.

  • Total Universal Set ($\varepsilon$): 600
  • All three ($S \cap T \cap F$): 40 (Given)
  • S & F only: 60 (Given: "swimming and football, but not tennis")
  • T & F only: 35 (Given: "tennis and football but not swimming")
  • S & T only: We are given that 55 liked swimming and tennis in total. This includes the 40 who like all three.
    $\rightarrow 55 - 40 = \mathbf{15}$
  • Swimming only (S only): Total S is 200. Subtract the overlaps.
    $\rightarrow 200 - (15 + 40 + 60) = 200 - 115 = \mathbf{85}$
  • Tennis only (T only): Total T is 250. Subtract the overlaps.
    $\rightarrow 250 - (15 + 40 + 35) = 250 - 90 = \mathbf{160}$
  • Football only (F only): Total F is 300. Subtract the overlaps.
    $\rightarrow 300 - (60 + 40 + 35) = 300 - 135 = \mathbf{165}$
  • None of the sports: Total students minus all the people inside the circles.
    $\rightarrow \text{Total inside circles} = 85 + 160 + 165 + 15 + 60 + 35 + 40 = 560$
    $\rightarrow \text{None} = 600 - 560 = \mathbf{40}$

(i) Completed Venn Diagram

(ii) How many students like at least one of the sports?

This asks for the union of all three sets ($S \cup T \cup F$), which is the sum of everyone inside the circles.

$$ 85 + 160 + 165 + 15 + 60 + 35 + 40 = \mathbf{560} $$

Answer: 560 students

(iii) How many students like none of the sports?

This is the number of students remaining outside all the circles (calculated in the preliminary step).

$$ \varepsilon - (S \cup T \cup F) = 600 - 560 = \mathbf{40} $$

Answer: 40 students

(iv) Number of students who like only one sport

We add together the outermost regions of each circle: those who like only Swimming, only Tennis, and only Football.

$$ \text{Only S} + \text{Only T} + \text{Only F} = 85 + 160 + 165 = \mathbf{410} $$

Answer: 410 students

(v) Number of students who like Tennis but not Football

We need everyone who is inside the Tennis circle ($T$) but outside the Football circle ($F'$). This consists of those who like "Tennis only" plus those who like "Swimming and Tennis only".

$$ n(T \cap F') = \text{Only T} + (\text{S & T only}) $$ $$ n(T \cap F') = 160 + 15 = \mathbf{175} $$

Answer: 175 students
