Probability Practice Question

In a school:

\[ P(M)=0.45 \]
\[ P(S)=0.35 \]
\[ P(M \cap S)=0.20 \]
  • \(M\): Student studies Mathematics
  • \(S\): Student studies Science
(a)(i) Find \(P(M \cup S)\)

Use the addition rule:

\[ P(M \cup S)=P(M)+P(S)-P(M \cap S) \]
\[ =0.45+0.35-0.20 \]
\[ =0.60 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.60} \]
(a)(ii) Find the probability that a student studies neither Mathematics nor Science.

Use the complement rule:

\[ P((M \cup S)')=1-P(M \cup S) \]
\[ =1-0.60 \]
\[ =0.40 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.40} \]
(a)(iii) Find the probability that a student studies Mathematics only.

Mathematics only means:

\[ P(M \cap S') \]
\[ =P(M)-P(M \cap S) \]
\[ =0.45-0.20 \]
\[ =0.25 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.25} \]
(a)(iv) Find the probability that a student studies exactly one of the two subjects.

Exactly one subject means:

\[ (M \cap S') \cup (S \cap M') \]

First find Science only:

\[ P(S \cap M')=P(S)-P(M \cap S) \]
\[ =0.35-0.20 \]
\[ =0.15 \]

Now add both exclusive regions:

\[ 0.25+0.15 \]
\[ =0.40 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.40} \]
(a)(v) Find \(P(M|S)\)

Use conditional probability:

\[ P(M|S)=\frac{P(M \cap S)}{P(S)} \]
\[ =\frac{0.20}{0.35} \]
\[ \approx 0.5714 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.5714} \]
(a)(vi) Find \(P(S|M)\)

Use conditional probability:

\[ P(S|M)=\frac{P(M \cap S)}{P(M)} \]
\[ =\frac{0.20}{0.45} \]
\[ \approx 0.4444 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.4444} \]
(b) Determine whether events \(M\) and \(S\) are independent.

Events are independent if:

\[ P(M \cap S)=P(M)\times P(S) \]
\[ P(M)\times P(S)=0.45 \times 0.35 \]
\[ =0.1575 \]

But:

\[ P(M \cap S)=0.20 \]

Since:

\[ 0.20 \ne 0.1575 \]

The events are not independent.

\[ \boxed{\text{M and S are not independent}} \]
