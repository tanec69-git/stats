Probability Practice Question

In a city:

\[ P(C)=0.40 \]
\[ P(B)=0.55 \]
\[ P(C \cup B)=0.75 \]
  • \(C\): Person owns a car
  • \(B\): Person owns a bicycle
  • \(M\): Person owns a motorcycle
(a)(i) Find \(P(C \cap B)\)

Use the addition rule:

\[ P(C \cup B)=P(C)+P(B)-P(C \cap B) \]
\[ 0.75=0.40+0.55-P(C \cap B) \]
\[ 0.75=0.95-P(C \cap B) \]
\[ P(C \cap B)=0.95-0.75 \]
\[ P(C \cap B)=0.20 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.20} \]
(a)(ii) Find the probability that a person owns neither a car nor a bicycle.

Use the complement rule:

\[ P((C \cup B)')=1-P(C \cup B) \]
\[ =1-0.75 \]
\[ =0.25 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.25} \]
(a)(iii) Find \(P(C|B)\)

Use conditional probability:

\[ P(C|B)=\frac{P(C \cap B)}{P(B)} \]
\[ =\frac{0.20}{0.55} \]
\[ \approx 0.3636 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.3636} \]
(a)(iv) Find \(P(B|C')\)

Step 1: Find \(P(C')\)

\[ P(C')=1-P(C) \]
\[ =1-0.40 \]
\[ =0.60 \]

Step 2: Find \(P(B \cap C')\)

\[ P(B \cap C')=P(B)-P(C \cap B) \]
\[ =0.55-0.20 \]
\[ =0.35 \]

Step 3: Apply conditional probability.

\[ P(B|C')=\frac{P(B \cap C')}{P(C')} \]
\[ =\frac{0.35}{0.60} \]
\[ \approx 0.5833 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.5833} \]
(b) Are events \(C\) and \(B\) mutually exclusive? Explain.

Two events are mutually exclusive if:

\[ P(C \cap B)=0 \]

But:

\[ P(C \cap B)=0.20 \]

Since the intersection is not zero, the events are not mutually exclusive.

\[ \boxed{\text{C and B are not mutually exclusive}} \]
(c) Are events \(C\) and \(B\) independent? Show working.

Two events are independent if:

\[ P(C \cap B)=P(C)\times P(B) \]
\[ P(C)\times P(B)=0.40 \times 0.55 \]
\[ =0.22 \]

But:

\[ P(C \cap B)=0.20 \]

Since:

\[ 0.20 \ne 0.22 \]

The events are not independent.

\[ \boxed{\text{C and B are not independent}} \]
(d) Suppose 60% of those who own both a car and a bicycle also own a motorcycle \((M)\).

This means:

\[ P(M|C \cap B)=0.60 \]
(e)(i) Find \(P(M \cap C \cap B)\)

Use conditional probability:

\[ P(M|C \cap B)=\frac{P(M \cap C \cap B)}{P(C \cap B)} \]
\[ 0.60=\frac{P(M \cap C \cap B)}{0.20} \]
\[ P(M \cap C \cap B)=0.60 \times 0.20 \]
\[ =0.12 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.12} \]
(e)(ii) Find \(P(M|C \cap B)\)

From part (d):

\[ P(M|C \cap B)=0.60 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.60} \]
