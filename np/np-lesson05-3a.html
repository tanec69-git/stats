Independent Events Probability Visualizer

\( A \) denotes the event that a student scores a distinction for his Additional Mathematics paper.

\( E \) denotes the event that a student scores a distinction for his Elementary Mathematics paper.

We are given:
\[ P(A)=0.25 \] \[ P(E)=0.56 \] \[ P(A \cap E)=0.14 \] \[ P(A|E)=0.25 \]
Determine whether event \( A \) is independent of event \( E \).

Explain your answer.

Method 1: Using Conditional Probability

Two events are independent if:
\[ P(A|E)=P(A) \]
From the question: \[ P(A|E)=0.25 \] and \[ P(A)=0.25 \] Since both values are equal: \[ P(A|E)=P(A) \] Therefore, event \( A \) is independent of event \( E \).

Method 2: Using the Multiplication Rule

Two events are independent if:
\[ P(A \cap E)=P(A)\times P(E) \]
Substitute the values: \[ P(A)\times P(E) = 0.25 \times 0.56 \] \[ = 0.14 \] But: \[ P(A \cap E)=0.14 \] Therefore: \[ P(A \cap E)=P(A)\times P(E) \] Hence, event \( A \) is independent of event \( E \).

Conclusion

\[ \boxed{ \text{Event } A \text{ is independent of event } E } \] because: \[ P(A|E)=P(A) \] and also \[ P(A \cap E)=P(A)\times P(E) \]
