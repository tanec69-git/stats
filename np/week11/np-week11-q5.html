Dice Game Analysis 🎲📊

5. A player throws an ordinary unbiased die, and if it is not a 6, his score X is the number on the face showing uppermost; if he throws a 6, he has a second throw and his score X is then the total obtained from his two throws. Calculate the expected value of the player's score X, and the expected number of throws.

🎯 Part 1: Calculate the expected value of the player's score X

First, let's establish the probability distribution for the score \( X \).

  • If the first throw is 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5, the game ends. The probability for each of these scores is \( \frac{1}{6} \).
  • If the first throw is a 6 (probability \( \frac{1}{6} \)), a second throw occurs. The final score will be 6 + the second throw (yielding 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, or 12). The probability for each of these scores is \( \frac{1}{6} \times \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{36} \).
Score \( x \) 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12
\( P(X=x) \) \( \frac{1}{6} \) (each) \( \frac{1}{36} \) (each)

Using the expected value formula: \( E(X) = \sum x \cdot P(X=x) \)

$$ \begin{aligned} E(X) &= \sum_{x=1}^{5} x \left(\frac{1}{6}\right) + \sum_{x=7}^{12} x \left(\frac{1}{36}\right) \\ E(X) &= (1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5)\left(\frac{1}{6}\right) + (7 + 8 + 9 + 10 + 11 + 12)\left(\frac{1}{36}\right) \\ E(X) &= (15)\left(\frac{1}{6}\right) + (57)\left(\frac{1}{36}\right) \\ E(X) &= \frac{15}{6} + \frac{57}{36} \\ E(X) &= \frac{90}{36} + \frac{57}{36} = \frac{147}{36} \\ E(X) &= \frac{49}{12} \approx 4.083 \end{aligned} $$

🎲 Part 2: Calculate the expected number of throws

Let \( T \) be the random variable representing the number of throws.

  • The player makes exactly 1 throw if they roll a 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 initially. So, \( P(T=1) = \frac{5}{6} \).
  • The player makes exactly 2 throws if they roll a 6 initially. So, \( P(T=2) = \frac{1}{6} \).

Using the expected value formula: \( E(T) = \sum t \cdot P(T=t) \)

$$ \begin{aligned} E(T) &= 1 \cdot P(T=1) + 2 \cdot P(T=2) \\ E(T) &= 1\left(\frac{5}{6}\right) + 2\left(\frac{1}{6}\right) \\ E(T) &= \frac{5}{6} + \frac{2}{6} \\ E(T) &= \frac{7}{6} \approx 1.167 \end{aligned} $$
