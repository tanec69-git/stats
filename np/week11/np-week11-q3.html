Expectation and Variance Analysis

3. Given \( X \) and \( Y \) are independent random variables and that \( E(X) = 5 \), \( Var(X) = 9 \), \( E(Y) = 4 \), \( Var(Y) = 16 \). Find:

(a) \( E(X^2) \)

Solution:

Using the variance formula: \( Var(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2 \)

$$ 9 = E(X^2) - (5)^2 $$

$$ 9 = E(X^2) - 25 $$

$$ E(X^2) = 9 + 25 $$

$$ E(X^2) = 34 $$

(b) Standard deviation, \( \sigma \) of \( X \)

Solution:

The standard deviation is the square root of the variance: \( \sigma_X = \sqrt{Var(X)} \)

$$ \sigma_X = \sqrt{9} $$

$$ \sigma_X = 3 $$

(c) \( E(5X + 2) \)

Solution:

Using the linearity of expectation property: \( E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b \)

$$ E(5X + 2) = 5E(X) + 2 $$

$$ E(5X + 2) = 5(5) + 2 $$

$$ E(5X + 2) = 25 + 2 $$

$$ E(5X + 2) = 27 $$

(d) \( E(5X + 2Y) \)

Solution:

Using the linearity of expectation for multiple variables: \( E(aX + bY) = aE(X) + bE(Y) \)

$$ E(5X + 2Y) = 5E(X) + 2E(Y) $$

$$ E(5X + 2Y) = 5(5) + 2(4) $$

$$ E(5X + 2Y) = 25 + 8 $$

$$ E(5X + 2Y) = 33 $$

(e) \( Var(5X + 2) \)

Solution:

Using the properties of variance: \( Var(aX + b) = a^2Var(X) \). Note that adding a constant (\( b \)) does not change the variance, but multiplying by a constant (\( a \)) scales the variance by \( a^2 \).

$$ Var(5X + 2) = 5^2 Var(X) $$

$$ Var(5X + 2) = 25(9) $$

$$ Var(5X + 2) = 225 $$
