Casino Game Analysis 🎰🪙

6. A casino has a game where a player tosses 3 fair coins. He wins $10 if 3 heads occur, $5 if 2 heads occur, $y if 1 head occurs and $2 if no head occurs.

Express in terms of y his expected gain from each game.
Given that he pays $4.50 to play each game, calculate:
(a) the value of y for which the game is fair.
(b) his expected gain or loss over 200 games if y = 2.50.

📝 Preliminary: Express his expected gain from each game in terms of y

First, we determine the probabilities for tossing 3 fair coins. The total number of possible outcomes is \( 2^3 = 8 \).

Number of Heads 3 2 1 0
Probability \( P(X=x) \) \( \frac{1}{8} \) \( \frac{3}{8} \) \( \frac{3}{8} \) \( \frac{1}{8} \)
Payout (Gain) $10 $5 $y $2

Expected gain \( E(G) = \sum (\text{Payout} \times \text{Probability}) \)

$$ \begin{aligned} E(G) &= 10\left(\frac{1}{8}\right) + 5\left(\frac{3}{8}\right) + y\left(\frac{3}{8}\right) + 2\left(\frac{1}{8}\right) \\ E(G) &= \frac{10}{8} + \frac{15}{8} + \frac{3y}{8} + \frac{2}{8} \\ E(G) &= \frac{27 + 3y}{8} \end{aligned} $$

⚖️ (a) Calculate the value of y for which the game is fair.

A game is considered "fair" when the expected gain from the game equals the cost to play the game. Therefore, the net expected profit is exactly $0.

Cost to play = $4.50

$$ \begin{aligned} E(G) &= 4.50 \\ \frac{27 + 3y}{8} &= 4.50 \\ 27 + 3y &= 36 \\ 3y &= 36 - 27 \\ 3y &= 9 \\ y &= 3 \end{aligned} $$

The value of y for a fair game is $3.00.

📉 (b) Calculate his expected gain or loss over 200 games if y = 2.50.

First, calculate the expected gross gain for a single game when \( y = 2.50 \).

$$ \begin{aligned} E(G) &= \frac{27 + 3(2.50)}{8} \\ E(G) &= \frac{27 + 7.50}{8} \\ E(G) &= \frac{34.50}{8} = \$4.3125 \end{aligned} $$

Now, calculate the expected net gain per game by subtracting the cost to play:

$$ \text{Net Gain per game} = E(G) - \text{Cost} $$

$$ \text{Net Gain per game} = 4.3125 - 4.50 = -\$0.1875 $$

Since the net gain is negative, the player expects to lose $0.1875 per game. Over 200 games:

$$ \text{Total expected net} = 200 \times (-0.1875) = -37.50 $$

His expected loss over 200 games is $37.50.
