Coin Flip Experiment Analysis

1. (a) Define the sample space for an experiment which involves 4 flips of a balanced coin.

Solution:

The sample space \( S \) for an experiment involving 4 flips of a balanced coin contains \( 2^4 = 16 \) possible outcomes. Let H represent Heads and T represent Tails. The complete sample space is:

$$ \begin{aligned} S = \{ & HHHH, HHHT, HHTH, HHTT, \\ & HTHH, HTHT, HTTH, HTTT, \\ & THHH, THHT, THTH, THTT, \\ & TTHH, TTHT, TTTH, TTTT \} \end{aligned} $$

(b) Let X be the number of heads. Represent the probability distribution f(x) = P(X=x) in a table form.

Solution:

The possible values for \( X \) (the number of heads) are 0, 1, 2, 3, and 4. By counting the number of outcomes for each scenario from the sample space above, we can determine the probability \( f(x) = P(X=x) \).

\( x \) (Number of Heads) Outcomes from Sample Space \( f(x) = P(X=x) \)
0 TTTT \( \frac{1}{16} \)
1 HTTT, THTT, TTHT, TTTH \( \frac{4}{16} = \frac{1}{4} \)
2 HHTT, HTHT, HTTH, THHT, THTH, TTHH \( \frac{6}{16} = \frac{3}{8} \)
3 HHHT, HHTH, HTHH, THHH \( \frac{4}{16} = \frac{1}{4} \)
4 HHHH \( \frac{1}{16} \)

(c) Calculate the expected value of X.

Solution:

The expected value \( E(X) \) is the sum of the product of each possible value of \( X \) and its corresponding probability.

$$ E(X) = \sum_{x=0}^{4} x \cdot P(X=x) $$

$$ E(X) = \left(0 \cdot \frac{1}{16}\right) + \left(1 \cdot \frac{4}{16}\right) + \left(2 \cdot \frac{6}{16}\right) + \left(3 \cdot \frac{4}{16}\right) + \left(4 \cdot \frac{1}{16}\right) $$

$$ E(X) = 0 + \frac{4}{16} + \frac{12}{16} + \frac{12}{16} + \frac{4}{16} $$

$$ E(X) = \frac{32}{16} $$

$$ E(X) = 2 $$
