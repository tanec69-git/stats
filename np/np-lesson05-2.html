There are 40 apples in a box. Some are New Zealand apples, some are not, some are good and some are rotten.

Let:

\[ N = \text{event that the apple is a New Zealand apple} \] \[ G = \text{event that the apple is a good apple} \] Evaluate the following probabilities.
New Zealand Other Countries
Good 16 14
Rotten 7 3
(i) \( P(N) \)

(ii) \( P(G') \)

(iii) \( P(N \cap G) \)

(iv) \( P(N' \cap G') \)

(v) \( P(G|N) \)

(vi) \( P(N'|G') \)

(i) \( P(N) \)

Probability that the apple is a New Zealand apple.

Number of New Zealand apples: \[ 16 + 7 = 23 \] Total apples: \[ 40 \] Therefore: \[ P(N) = \frac{23}{40} \]

(ii) \( P(G') \)

\( G' \) means the apple is not good, which means the apple is rotten.

Number of rotten apples: \[ 7 + 3 = 10 \] Therefore: \[ P(G') = \frac{10}{40} \] \[ = \frac{1}{4} \]

(iii) \( P(N \cap G) \)

\( N \cap G \) means:

New Zealand apple AND good apple.

Number of apples satisfying both conditions: \[ 16 \] Therefore: \[ P(N \cap G) = \frac{16}{40} \] \[ = \frac{2}{5} \]

(iv) \( P(N' \cap G') \)

\( N' \cap G' \) means:

Not a New Zealand apple AND not good.

This corresponds to rotten apples from other countries.

Number of such apples: \[ 3 \] Therefore: \[ P(N' \cap G') = \frac{3}{40} \]

(v) \( P(G|N) \)

Probability that the apple is good given that it is from New Zealand.

Formula: \[ P(G|N) = \frac{P(G \cap N)}{P(N)} \] Number of good New Zealand apples: \[ 16 \] Total New Zealand apples: \[ 23 \] Therefore: \[ P(G|N) = \frac{16}{23} \]

(vi) \( P(N'|G') \)

Probability that the apple is not from New Zealand given that it is rotten.

Formula: \[ P(N'|G') = \frac{P(N' \cap G')}{P(G')} \] Rotten apples from other countries: \[ 3 \] Total rotten apples: \[ 10 \] Therefore: \[ P(N'|G') = \frac{3}{10} \]

Verification Using Conditional Probability Formula

(i)

\[ P(G|N) = \frac{P(N \cap G)}{P(N)} \]
Substitute the values from part (a): \[ = \frac{\frac{16}{40}}{\frac{23}{40}} \] \[ = \frac{16}{40} \times \frac{40}{23} \] \[ = \frac{16}{23} \] Hence verified: \[ P(G|N)=\frac{16}{23} \]

(ii)

\[ P(N'|G') = \frac{P(N' \cap G')}{P(G')} \]
Substitute the values from part (a): \[ = \frac{\frac{3}{40}}{\frac{10}{40}} \] \[ = \frac{3}{40} \times \frac{40}{10} \] \[ = \frac{3}{10} \] Hence verified: \[ P(N'|G')=\frac{3}{10} \]
