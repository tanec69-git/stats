32% of the undergraduates in a University read The Business Times every day, 48% of them read The Straits Times every day, and 9% of them read both papers every day.

What is the percentage of students who read one or more newspapers published by SPH every day?

(Assume that SPH only publishes these two English newspapers.)

Step 1: Use the Union Formula

To find the percentage of students who read one or more newspapers: \[ P(B \cup S) = P(B) + P(S) - P(B \cap S) \] where: \[ P(B) = 32\% \] \[ P(S) = 48\% \] \[ P(B \cap S) = 9\% \]

Step 2: Substitute the Values

\[ P(B \cup S) = 32\% + 48\% - 9\% \] \[ = 80\% - 9\% \] \[ = 71\% \]

Final Answer

Therefore: \[ P(B \cup S) = 71\% \] So, 71% of the students read one or more SPH newspapers every day.
