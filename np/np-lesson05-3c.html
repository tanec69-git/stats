Conditional Probability Practice

Given:

\[ P(M)=0.5 \]
\[ P(N)=0.3 \]
\[ P(M \cup N)=0.69 \]
(i) Find \(P(M|N)\)

Step 1: Find \(P(M \cap N)\)

\[ P(M \cup N)=P(M)+P(N)-P(M \cap N) \]
\[ 0.69=0.5+0.3-P(M \cap N) \]
\[ 0.69=0.8-P(M \cap N) \]
\[ P(M \cap N)=0.8-0.69 \]
\[ P(M \cap N)=0.11 \]

Step 2: Use conditional probability

\[ P(M|N)=\frac{P(M \cap N)}{P(N)} \]
\[ =\frac{0.11}{0.3} \]
\[ \approx 0.3667 \]

Answer:

\[ \boxed{0.3667} \]
(ii) Explain whether \(M\) and \(N\) are independent.

Two events are independent if:

\[ P(M \cap N)=P(M)\times P(N) \]
\[ P(M)\times P(N)=0.5 \times 0.3 \]
\[ =0.15 \]

But:

\[ P(M \cap N)=0.11 \]

Since:

\[ 0.11 \ne 0.15 \]

The events are not independent.

\[ \boxed{\text{M and N are not independent}} \]
(iii) Explain whether \(M\) and \(N\) are mutually exclusive.

Two events are mutually exclusive if:

\[ P(M \cap N)=0 \]

But:

\[ P(M \cap N)=0.11 \]

Since the intersection is not zero, the events are not mutually exclusive.

\[ \boxed{\text{M and N are not mutually exclusive}} \]
