5(a) List the sample space for a throw of a balanced dice.

(b) If we throw a dice once, find the probability of getting:
  1. a “1”
  2. a “3” or “5”
  3. an odd number
(c) If we throw a dice twice, find the probability of getting:
  1. a “1” followed by a “2”
  2. a “2” or “6” followed by an odd number
  3. a total score of 4
  4. a total score of at least 11

5(a) Sample Space

\[ S = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\} \]

5(b) One Throw

(i) Getting a “1”

\[ P(1)=\frac{1}{6} \]
Probability = 1/6

(ii) Getting a “3” or “5”

\[ P(3 \text{ or } 5)=\frac{2}{6} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{3} \]
Probability = 1/3

(iii) Getting an Odd Number

Odd numbers are: \[ \{1,3,5\} \]
\[ P(\text{odd})=\frac{3}{6} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{2} \]
Probability = 1/2

5(c) Two Throws

(i) Getting a “1” followed by a “2”

\[ P(1 \text{ then } 2) = \frac{1}{6}\times\frac{1}{6} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{36} \]
Probability = 1/36

(ii) Getting a “2” or “6” followed by an odd number

First throw: \[ \{2,6\} \] Second throw: \[ \{1,3,5\} \]
\[ P= \frac{2}{6}\times\frac{3}{6} \]
\[ =\frac{6}{36} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{6} \]
Probability = 1/6

(iii) Getting a Total Score of 4

Possible outcomes: \[ (1,3),(2,2),(3,1) \]
\[ P(\text{total }4)=\frac{3}{36} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{12} \]
Probability = 1/12

(iv) Getting a Total Score of at Least 11

Possible outcomes: \[ (5,6),(6,5),(6,6) \]
\[ P(\text{at least }11)=\frac{3}{36} \]
\[ =\frac{1}{12} \]
Probability = 1/12
